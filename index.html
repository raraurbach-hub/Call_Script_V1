Preparation Before You Pick Up

You are the first impression of a physician-led, premium practice. Every call should feel like a concierge service — calm, confident, knowledgeable. You are not selling. You are guiding a qualified patient toward the right next step.

Goal of every call:

  • 1Capture contact information
  • 2Understand the patient's condition and intent
  • 3Educate briefly and clearly
  • 4Book a telemedicine consultation with Dr. Choxi
Step 0 Opening
Office
"Good morning / afternoon, this is [Name] with STEMS Health. How can I help you today?"
Step 1 Capture Contact Information First

As soon as the patient identifies why they're calling, before going any further:

Office
"I'd love to help with that. Before we go any further, may I get your name, best email address, and a phone number — just in case we get disconnected?"
Enter into Jane App: Full name, DOB, phone, address, email, sex.
Step 2 Understand the Patient

Once information is captured, ask:

Office
"Can you tell me a little more about what you're dealing with — is this a specific joint or area, and do you have any imaging on file, like an MRI or X-ray?"
Office
"And have you tried any other treatments so far — injections, surgery, physical therapy?"
Listen for: Area of concern, chronicity, imaging status, prior treatments, how they heard about STEMS.
Step 3 Education Block

Use the section below that matches what the patient is asking. Keep it conversational — one or two points at a time. You do not need to recite everything.

What We Do — Simple Overview
Office
"STEMS Health is a regenerative medicine practice based in Miami Beach. We specialize in non-surgical, physician-administered treatments designed to support the body's own healing process — rather than masking pain or removing tissue. Everything is performed by Dr. Ankeet Choxi, who has deep experience with both orthopedic and aesthetic regenerative cases."
The Science — Plain Language

Use when a patient asks: "What is a stem cell?" / "What's the difference between PRP and stem cells?" / "What are MUSE cells?"

MSCs (Stem Cells)
"Mesenchymal stem cells are your body's repair crew. Found in bone marrow, fat tissue, and umbilical cord. They work primarily by sending out signals that calm inflammation and encourage healing. Think of them as coaches — they direct everything around them."
MUSEcells™
"MUSEcells are a rare, specialized population found within those MSCs. What makes them different is that they're stress-activated — when the body is under duress, they respond. They have the potential to become multiple tissue types: nerve, muscle, cartilage, skin. Rather than just coaching, they can step in and play. We are the only practice in the region offering them."
PRP
"PRP is drawn from the patient's own blood, spun down to concentrate growth factors, and injected directly into the affected area. It's a strong anti-inflammatory tool — effective alone or as a complement to cell-based therapy."
BMAC
"BMAC is bone marrow aspirate concentrate — we draw from the patient's own iliac crest, concentrate the regenerative cells, and use it at the treatment site. It's an autologous option, meaning it comes from you."
The Benefits
Office
"Regenerative therapy is not a guaranteed fix, and we're always transparent about that. What it offers is a meaningful, non-surgical path for patients who haven't responded to conventional treatment — or who want to avoid surgery altogether. Most patients report reduced pain, improved function, and in many cases, durable results over months and years."
Office
"Recovery is typically minimal — most patients return to normal activity within days to a couple of weeks depending on the protocol. This is very different from a surgical timeline."

No embryos are involved. Donors are thoroughly screened for infectious disease and health history before donation is accepted."

Step 4 Handling Common Questions
"Is the lab FDA-registered and compliant?"
"Yes. The lab we work with is FDA-registered and operates under cGMP standards. All processing and testing follows federal guidelines for human cellular and tissue-based products under 21 CFR Part 1271. We only work with labs that meet that standard."
"How do I know the cells are actually alive and viable when injected?"
"Every batch is tested for cell viability before release — we require high viability thresholds before accepting any product. The cells are cryopreserved using validated protocols and shipped under temperature-controlled conditions. By the time they reach the treatment room, they've been confirmed viable and are thawed precisely on the day of your procedure."
"Where do the cells come from, and how are donors screened?"
"The cells come from umbilical cord tissue — Wharton's jelly — donated by healthy mothers after planned caesarean births. Donors are screened for a full panel of infectious diseases, including HIV and hepatitis, in line with FDA requirements. Medical and family history is reviewed. Only donors who pass that screening are accepted."
"What testing or certifications does the lab have?"
"The lab holds FDA registration and operates under cGMP protocols. Each batch undergoes sterility testing, identity confirmation, and viability analysis before release. Dr. Choxi is happy to address the specifics during your consultation."
"Has this product been used in published research or clinical trials?"
"Yes. MSCs from Wharton's jelly have been studied extensively in peer-reviewed literature across orthopedic, neurological, and inflammatory conditions. MUSEcells specifically have been the subject of clinical trials in Japan. Dr. Choxi can speak to the evidence base relevant to your specific condition."
"How is the product stored and transported? Where does it come from?"
"The cells are cryopreserved and shipped in validated, temperature-controlled containers by logistics partners experienced in biological transport. On the day of your procedure, the cells are thawed under precise conditions in the office. The entire cold chain is monitored and documented."
"Can I see the Certificate of Analysis for my batch?"
"Yes — a Certificate of Analysis is available for every product we use. It documents the cell count, viability, sterility results, and identity markers for that specific lot. It's a reasonable request and one we're fully comfortable fulfilling."

Reference Objection Quick Reference
Objection Response
"Is this covered by insurance?" Regenerative therapies are generally not covered. Most patients find the investment worthwhile given the avoidance of surgery or long-term medication. We're happy to walk you through pricing during the consultation.
"I've heard stem cells don't work." A fair concern — the field has a lot of variation in quality. What sets us apart is physician administration, lab-grade sourcing, and a protocol tailored to your specific condition. Dr. Choxi can show you the evidence base for your case.
"Can I just come in without a consultation?" Dr. Choxi requires a consultation first — imaging review is essential to building the right protocol. We don't offer walk-in treatments, and that's by design.
"Is this experimental?" The therapies we use are derived from well-studied cell populations with significant peer-reviewed literature. Dr. Choxi will be direct with you about what the evidence supports for your condition.
